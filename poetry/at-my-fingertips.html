At My Fingertips

What is it about

the town of Riva San Vitale

that makes the heart long

for something more?

Is it the wind that howls,

or the water that’s

always a distance away,

or the mountains that

tower above you,

next to you, guarding you,

guiding you, perhaps?

I walk along here by Lake Lugano,

and I see a future for myself

that’s possible because

I’m here—in this program.

It’s possible because

I have the opportunity to explore places

that allow me to be truly myself,

that allow me to learn about my heritage,

that allow me to research something

that actually means the world to me,

and here the world is all around me.

This place is so different

yet touched by the same problems

that are touched by other countries.

We’re all one people, not the same,

but we’re all human—we’re all living

and breathing the same air,

becoming ever more globalized

and interconnected.

The Swiss flag only is visible

because of the breeze or the wind

that it’s caught in the midst of.

And I find it interesting

that it’s a square flag—

it’s symmetrical on all sides,

which I think really symbolizes

the unity of the cantons here,

the unity of people

that speak different languages,

that have different customs

and yet are able to work together.

I’m not trying to say

that Switzerland is perfect;

every country has its flaws.

But I do know that

we can learn something

from each country

because the way

things are in one country

don’t have to remain the same.

They won’t remain the same:

Over time things will progress,

and that’s just a fact.

So, I do believe in change.

I do believe that the United States

and other countries

can progress towards

a more humane society,

towards a society that truly cares

for the children and the elderly,

for anyone who is marginalized,

for its entire people, really,

because we’re not a people

unless we care about each generation.

Just because some people

don’t or sometimes can’t work

doesn’t mean that

they don’t have value

or something to contribute.

Not everything’s about money,

and—more importantly—

not everything is about fleeting pleasure,

especially the kind that scars others.

People are not objects or machines.

In the end, I just want a society

that actively looks after people

even if they’re not in your circle,

even if they’re not in crisis,

even if they’re not on the brink.

I just want people to care

even when it doesn’t mean

life or death. I know we can.


February 21, 2022
