At My Fingertips

What is it about Riva San Vitale

that makes the heart long for something more?

Is it the wind that howls,

or the water that’s always a distance away,

or the mountains that tower above you,

next to you, guarding you?

I walk along here by Lake Lugano,

and I see a future for myself

that is only possible because

I am here. It’s only possible because

I have the opportunity to explore places

that allow me to be truly myself,

that allow me to get in touch with my heritage,

that allow me to to research something

that actually means the world to me,

because here the world is all around me.

This place is so different

yet touched by the same problems

that are touched by citizens of other countries.

We’re all one people, not the same,

but we’re all human; we’re all living

and breathing the same air.

The Swiss flag only is visible

because of the breeze or the wind

that it’s caught in the middle of.

And I find it interesting

that it’s a square flag:

It’s symmetrical on all sides,

and because of that I think

it really symbolizes

the unity of the cantons here,

the unity of people

that speak different languages,

that have different cultures

and yet work together.

I’m not trying to say

that Switzerland is perfect,

because every country has its flaws.

But I do know that we can learn something

from each country

because the way things are in one country

do not have to remain the same.

In fact, they won’t remain the same.

Over time things will progress,

and that’s just a fact.

So I do believe in change.

I do believe that the United States

and other countries

can progress towards

a more humane society,

towards a society that actually cares

for the children and the elderly

and its entire people

because we’re not a people

unless we care about each generation.

Just because the children and the elderly

don’t or usually can’t work doesn’t mean

that they don’t have value

or something to contribute.

Not everything’s about money.

Not everything’s about how much

you can spend or save.

In the end, I just want a society

that actually looks after people

even if they’re not in your community,

even if they’re not in crisis,

even if they’re not at the brink.

I just want people to care

even when it doesn’t mean

life or death.


February 21, 2022
